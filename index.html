üîç Key Findings

Finding #1: Frequency Disparity

Annual members took 341,906 rides compared to just 23,163 rides by casual riders‚Äîa 15x difference. This demonstrates that members rely on the service as their primary transportation mode, while casual riders use it sporadically.

Finding #2: Duration Patterns

Casual riders averaged 35.28 minutes per trip versus 11.31 minutes for members. The 3x longer duration indicates leisure-oriented usage (sightseeing, recreation) versus utility-focused trips (commuting, errands).

Finding #3: Weekly Usage Patterns

Members peaked on weekdays (Thursday) with consistent usage Monday-Friday, while casual riders peaked on weekends (Saturday). This confirms members are commuters while casual riders are leisure users and tourists.

üíª SQL Code Sample

Example query analyzing ride patterns by user type and day:

SELECT day_name, usertype, COUNT(*) AS total_rides, ROUND(AVG(ride_length_min), 2) AS avg_ride_minutes FROM `cyclistic-analysis.bike_data.trips_2019_q1` WHERE ride_length_min > 1 AND ride_length_min <= 1440 GROUP BY day_name, usertype ORDER BY CASE day_name WHEN 'Sunday' THEN 1 WHEN 'Monday' THEN 2 WHEN 'Tuesday' THEN 3 WHEN 'Wednesday' THEN 4 WHEN 'Thursday' THEN 5 WHEN 'Friday' THEN 6 WHEN 'Saturday' THEN 7 END;

üí° Strategic Recommendations

1. Weekend Membership Promotions

Launch targeted marketing campaigns on Saturdays when casual riders are most active. Offer time-limited membership discounts and demonstrate ROI through cost comparison tools.

2. Reposition for Short-Trip Commuting

Shift messaging from "leisure service" to "practical transportation." Showcase member testimonials highlighting time and cost savings for daily commutes.

3. Implement Trial Membership Pass

Create a 10-ride or 30-day trial pass as a stepping stone between single rides and annual membership, reducing conversion friction and building customer confidence.

üìà Expected Business Impact

Converting just 10% of casual riders (2,316 customers) to annual memberships would significantly increase revenue and customer lifetime value. Given that annual members are substantially more profitable than per-ride customers, this conversion strategy directly supports the company's growth objectives.
