Customer Behavior Analysis for Marketing Strategy Optimization

üìä Project Overview

This analysis examined behavioral differences between annual members and casual riders in a bike-share program to develop targeted marketing strategies for membership conversion. By analyzing 365,000+ bike trips, the project identified distinct usage patterns that inform data-driven recommendations for increasing profitable annual memberships.

üîç Key Findings

Finding #1: Dramatic Frequency Disparity

Annual members take 15x more rides than casual riders.

  • Subscribers (Annual Members): 341,906 rides (93.7% of total)
  • Customers (Casual Riders): 23,163 rides (6.3% of total)
  • Ratio demonstrates members rely on service as primary transportation

Insight: Annual members utilize the bike-share service far more frequently, suggesting they rely on it as a primary mode of transportation rather than occasional use.

Finding #2: Contrasting Trip Durations

Casual riders take 3x longer trips than annual members.

  • Casual Riders: 35.28 minutes average trip length
  • Annual Members: 11.31 minutes average trip length
  • Longer duration suggests leisure-oriented vs. utility-focused usage

Insight: The longer ride duration for casual riders suggests leisure-oriented usage (sightseeing, recreation) versus the shorter, utility-focused trips of annual members (commuting, errands).

Finding #3: Distinct Weekly Patterns

Usage patterns differ dramatically by day of week.

  • Annual Members: Peak on weekdays (Thursday highest)
  • Casual Riders: Peak on weekends (Saturday highest)
  • Weekend vs. weekday split reveals commuter vs. tourist behavior

Insight: Subscribers demonstrate commuter behavior with consistent weekday usage, while casual riders show tourist/leisure behavior concentrated on weekends.

üí° Strategic Recommendations

1. Weekend Membership Promotions

Launch Saturday/Sunday focused campaigns targeting the 23,163 casual riders when they're actively using the service. Offer limited-time discounts and highlight cost savings: "Rode 4+ times this month? A membership pays for itself!"

2. Reposition for Daily Commuting

Shift marketing from "leisure service" to "practical transportation." Create campaign: "Not Just for Long Rides - Perfect for Your Daily Commute." Showcase member testimonials and demonstrate ROI with cost comparison calculator.

3. Trial Membership Pass

Introduce 10-ride or 30-day "Test Drive Membership" pass as stepping stone between single rides and annual commitment. Include automatic upgrade offer to reduce conversion friction and create natural pathway to full membership.

Expected Business Impact

Converting 10% of casual riders (2,316 customers) to annual memberships would substantially increase revenue and customer lifetime value, given that annual members are significantly more profitable than per-ride customers.

üîß Methodology

Data Processing

Utilized Google BigQuery for SQL-based analysis. Added calculated fields for ride duration (minutes) and day of week to enable temporal pattern analysis.

Data Cleaning

  • Removed rides shorter than 1 minute (false starts/errors)
  • Removed rides longer than 1,440 minutes (24 hours - lost/stolen bikes)
  • Cleaned 192 invalid records (0.05% of dataset)
  • Final clean dataset: 364,877 valid trips

Analysis Approach

Employed SQL aggregations (COUNT, AVG), GROUP BY user type and temporal dimensions, and comparative analysis to identify behavioral patterns between subscriber segments.

Visualization

Created pivot tables and charts in Google Sheets showing ride frequency by day of week and average ride duration by user type, optimized for stakeholder presentation.
