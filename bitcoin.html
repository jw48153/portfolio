üîç Key Findings

Finding #1: Volume-Volatility Correlation

Trading volume increases 19x from low to extreme volatility periods.

  • Low Volatility (< 2%): $7.6M average volume
  • Medium Volatility (2-5%): $37.9M average volume (43% of all days)
  • High Volatility (5-10%): $88.8M average volume
  • Extreme Volatility (> 10%): $144.5M average volume

Insight: Extreme price movements attract massive trading participation. Market participants significantly increase activity during periods of uncertainty through panic selling or FOMO buying.

Finding #2: Bear Markets Show Higher Volatility

Bear markets exhibit 9% higher volatility than bull markets despite shorter duration.

  • Bull Markets: 1,048 days (40%) | 6.20% volatility | $79.8M volume
  • Consolidation: 1,007 days (38%) | 3.94% volatility | $41.7M volume
  • Bear Markets: 596 days (22%) | 6.76% volatility | $60.4M volume

Insight: Fear-driven selling creates more erratic price movements than greed-driven buying. While bull markets dominate in duration and volume, bear markets compress into shorter, more volatile periods.

Finding #3: Traditional Market Hours Dominate

Despite 24/7 availability, trading follows business hours with 28.8% volume premium.

  • Peak Volume: 14:00 UTC (9 AM US EST) - US market open
  • Lowest Volume: 07:00 UTC (2 AM US EST) - deep sleep hours
  • Business Hours (9:00-18:00 UTC): 28.8% higher than off-hours
  • Monday shows highest weekly volume (weekend news buildup)

Insight: Professional traders and institutional investors drive the majority of Bitcoin market activity. Human behavior patterns persist even in decentralized 24/7 markets.

üí° Strategic Recommendations

1. Optimize Trade Timing

Execute large trades during 9:00-18:00 UTC for optimal liquidity. Peak activity at 14:00 UTC (9 AM EST) provides best price execution with 28.8% higher volume than off-hours.

2. Phase-Based Risk Management

Tighten stop-losses during bear markets due to 9% higher volatility. Increase position sizes during bull markets when 32% higher volume supports better exits.

3. Volume-Based Volatility Monitoring

Set volume-based alerts at 2x-3x average daily levels. Volume surges signal heightened volatility, providing early warning for position adjustments.

üîß Methodology

Data Processing

Utilized Google BigQuery for large-scale SQL analysis, employing window functions (LAG) for time-series comparisons and CASE statements for market phase classification.

Key Calculations

  • Daily Volatility: ((high - low) / open) * 100
  • 30-day Price Change: Trend identification using LAG function
  • Market Phases: Bull (>10% gain), Bear (<-10% loss), Consolidation (-10% to +10%)
  • Temporal Analysis: Hour and day-of-week extraction for pattern identification

Visualization

Created comprehensive charts in Google Sheets optimized for each insight, using column charts for categorical comparisons and line charts for temporal patterns.
